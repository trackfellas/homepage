Trip Details

  • Destination Overview

    Country: Cuba
    Location: Carribean
    Languages: Spanish
    Climate: Steady temperatures all year round with day temperatures from 26°C in winter up to humid 32°C in summer. Hurricanes can hit Cuba from June until November, most often in September
    Seasons: Cuba can be visited all year. Best season is from November until May with lots of sun and temperatures around 25°C during the day and 20°C at night.
    Travel period: June - October is off season and December - May is the main season, however Cuba is not overcrowded. This might change with the improvement of relations between the USA and Cuba, which could lead to a greater number of tourists from the US. Currently direct tourist travel from the USA to Cuba is not possible.
    Visa: A visa/tourist card for EU travelers is valid for 30 days and can be renewed for another 30 days. You can get it at the Cuban embassy or most airlines or travel agents also sell it to you before departure. Price is 25 CUC. EU citizens also need a passport, that is valid for at least 6 more months. For US tourists visiting Cuba is still forbidden by US law.
    Currency conversion: 1 CUC = 0,95 € (April 2015). The convertible peso (CUC) is the currency for tourists in Cuba and is equivalent to the US Dollar.
    Accommodation: Every house with a blue anchor in front can be rented by tourists. Houses with a red anchor are just for locals. Prices for accomodation can normally be negotiated in Casas Particulares.
    Health Care: No special vaccination necessary, apart from tetanus and diphtheria. No malaria, but dengue fever occurs. Medical care is quite good. Nevertheless there is a general lack of medication in pharmacies, so better bring your own first-aid kit.
    Travel Warning: No travel warnings. Cuban citizens need a permission to make private business. Accommodation in private households without official permission might lead to problems with the police for the host and the guest, so better check the documents in advance.

  • Havana

    Type: Big city, capital
    Date: 03.04. - 07.04.15
    Comment: Is a MUST see. It's beautiful and I would say the most important city BUT it is the dirtiest city I have seen in Cuba and also the smelliest.


    Accommodation: Walkiria's House
    Type: Holiday Apartment
    Room: Middle-class (BnB/AC/TV/Private Bath)
    Cost: 25 CUC // 23 €
    Comment: The apartment is nice for cuban standards. The house is very safe with a security guard at the front door. There is a beautiful view from the bed room over old Havana and the sea including the sunset. Lots of things are in walking distance and you are just 3 minutes away from the Malecon.


    Food: La Torre
    Type: Restaurant & Bar
    Comment: Nice 360° view over Havana; Good food and reasonable price for tourists.

    Entertainment: Casa de la Musica
    Type: Club
    Cost: 10 CUC-40 CUC // 9€-37€
    Comment: Live music & concerts, often latin american dances


    Sightseeing: Morro Castle
    Type: Historical sight
    Transportation: Private taxi
    Comment: Morro castle was built during the late 16th century. Especially its lighthouse is a symbol of Havana. If you come after 4 pm entry is $8 CUC but entitles you to stay and watch the cannons being fired before sunset.


    Sightseeing: Santa Maria
    Type: Beach
    Transportation: Public Transportation
    Comment: Nice beach 20 minutes away from the center of Havana

  • Trinidad

    Type: Town
    Date: 07.04. - 10.4.15
    Comment: Very beautiful and historical, old city, many tourists, many restaurants, good (international) food, great partys (if you like salsa), very cheap. Around 30 minutes away from Trinidad there is a beautiful beach called Playa Ancón.
    Tip: You don't need to book in advance a Casa Particular, there are many people waiting for you at the bus station!


    Accommodation: Hostal Anita y Pirolo
    Type: Holiday Apartment
    Room: Middle-class (BnB/AC/TV/Private Bath)
    Cost: 15 CUC // 14 €
    Comment: We didn't book a hostel. We met Anita while she was looking for new people at the bus station. The house was 10 min. walking distance from the city center. The apartment was very beautiful with a separate entry, terrace, roof top, 2 rooms and space for 3 people.
    Tip: The normal price is 25 CUC.

    Comment: It's outside, daily program, live music, dancing classes


    Sightseeing: Historic Center

  • Santiago de Cuba

    Type: City
    Date: 12.04. - 14.04.15
    Comment: For transportation in the city center you can use the motorbike-taxis for just 0,50 CUC, it's super cheap.
    Tip: The sun is VERY strong. Wake up in the early morning to do sightseeing, or in the late afternoon. There is also a train connection from Santiago de Cuba over Camagüey and Santa Clara to Havana every few days, which takes about 16 hours overnight and might be a nice alternative to travel with locals. Prices between 63 CUC and 30 CUC are reasonable, but you will have to buy the tickets up to 5 days in advance from the station your are leaving from. Bring your passport. For more infos check out: Seat61: How to book

    Accommodation: Casa Margarita
    Type: B&B
    Room: Shared Apartment
    Cost: 20 CUC // 18 €
    Comment: Margarita is a very nice person, the apartment is situated in an old colonial house, the room is beautiful but without a window.

    Sightseeing: Santa Ifigenia Cemetery
    Type: Historical sight, Walking tour
    Transportation: Taxi
    Price: Free
    Comment: If you have time you should visit the cemetery. It's in the northwest of Santiago de Cuba. Take a nice walk down to the harbour and catch a bicycle-taxi. You will pass the train station and the old "Bacardi-factory". One of the most visited places in the churchyard is the big Mausoleum that keeps the ashes of one of the most important National Heros "José Martí".

    Sightseeing: Antonio Maceo Revolution Square
    Type: Historical sight
    Transportation: By foot
    Comment: This square is very close to the Viazul bus station and here you can find the highest statue in the whole country in honor of the hero Antonio Maceo Grajale

    Sightseeing: Cathedral Asunción
    Type: Historical sight, city center
    Transportation: By foot
    Comment: The Square around the cathedral is very famous and beautiful. Here you can find a little park, the town hall and the oldest house from Cuba (build in 1516).

    Sightseeing: Cuartel Moncada
    Type: Museum
    Transportation: by foot
    Cost: 4 €
    Comment: This is where the history of the Cuban revolution starts. A must see!

  • Guantanamo

    Type: Town
    Date: 14.04. - 18.04.15
    Comment: Guantanamo is very cute and nice to see for a stopover, there are no tourists, VERY cheap, Pizza for 1 CUC, Mojitos 1,50 CUC. Most prices are in CUP (national money). There is really not that much to do or see. I visited the family of my friend. I stayed in a casa particulares for locals, but you will find nice Bed&Breakfast in the center.
    Tip: Better head east to isolated Baracoa with its rich nature, beaches and the National Park Alejandro de Humboldt.

    Food: Los Ensueños
    Type: Restaurant
    Comment: The restaurant is directly in the small center. There’s no sign, just a nude statue denoting the entrance to Guantánamo’s allegedly best restaurant. Very beautiful interior, nice and well tasted food. If you are lucky you can even choose between chicken, lobster and fish in different sauces. And you find here one of the best mojitos in Cuba for just 1CUC.
    Tip: House special is the chicken 'Gordon Blue'
